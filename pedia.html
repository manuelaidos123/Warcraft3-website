Race Strategies, Pros and Cons

How To Basic: Human

Human units for the most part are fragile and without much brute force, but they take advantage of carefully developed technology and magic, to bring foes to their knees.

Advantages

  • Great expanding possibilities

    With the peasants ability to do cooperative building, you can build a Town Hall very quickly compared to other races. With masonry upgrades from the Lumber Mill you can also create a defense with towers that is difficult to penetrate for the opponent. While Human expansions are costly and therefore quite a commitment, they are often the key to unlocking your powerful tech-tree.

  • Militia creeping

    Another very useful function peasants have is, that they can be called to arms and become militia. Although they are quite vulnerable, they deal as much damage as a footman, so pulling a couple of them will allow to immediately creep big camps and thus getting a level and item advantage right off the bat.

  • Broad spectrum of possibilities

    Every single Human unit sees use. This makes it possible to hard counter most of what your opponent throws at you. Though it might seem overwhelming to learn so many different compositions for newer players, I think it have some clear benefits. Races such as Orc and Undead can use the same unit compositions in almost all games. While that is easy to grasp, it also makes it very difficult to see where you can improve your games compared to Human who will have to learn transitioning with very drastic changes throughout the games.

  • Staff of Sanctuary

    This is a super powerful item, and vastly underrated by many of the new players I have talked to. Giving Human players the possibility to salvage heroes and units from a fight and healing them up fully is simply great.

Disadvantages

  • Fragile units

    If a Human army gets caught on the wrong foot, unprepared without a Scroll of Teleportation, the opponent can quickly tear the army apart, due to the units' low durability.

  • Can be bullied early game

    Having an enemy hero interrupting your creeping or expansion can be very frustrating to play against as a Human. In the early game where you only have your hero and a couple of footmen, your only option will often be to flee from a Blademaster attacking you.

How To Basic: Orc

Advantages

  • Hard to kill

    Orc has a natural hardiness with high hit point units, complemented by many spells and abilities that sustain fights and keep the Orc army healthy. They also benefit from a shared armor upgrade for all units, which helps encourage a play style of long drawn out battles.

  • Strong racial hero options

    All the Orc heroes are incredibly powerful and are all used in several different scenarios. Orc is arguably the race that has the strongest tri-hero combination.

  • Voodoo language

    Every item in the Orc shop is invaluable and mends Orc's weaknesses well. Efficient healing with salves, the unique Tiny Great Hall for late game expansions, a powerful Orb and the incredible speed scroll which too often goes underappreciated by newer players.

  • Strong presence at Tier 2

    All but one of the Orc units are available at Tier 2, which allows them the unique ability to field their late game army early on. This entails them hitting a strong power spike once their Tier 2 units take the field, giving them a good map presence.

  • Loads of lock-down

    Orcs have a ton of ways to lock down enemy units, which especially ties well with Blademasters high damage output. Between War Stomp, Hex, Ensnare and Orb of Lightning you can be sure to have ways of pinning down the opponent.

Disadvantages

  • Weak late game powerhouse unit

    The other races all have strong Tier 3 units that, if permitted, can be devastating in high population battles. Orc has the Tauren which, while strong on the ground, is easily crowd controlled or focused by the other races' big hitters such as Frost Wyrms, Gryphon Riders or Chimeras.

  • Vulnerable key buildings

    Any player who has a few Orc games under their belt has keenly felt the pain of their burrows being destroyed, delaying their second hero or first Tier 2 units. They are the only race who have a supply building that does not have fortified armor, making them ripe for the picking.

  • No magic immunity / weak anti-magic

    Orc is the only race with no magic immune units. This leads to the massing of spells or magic damage being quite effective, as we see with the commonly used mass casters by Night Elf (Talons) and Human (Sorceresses/Priests).

  • Limited anti-air options

    Orcs deal well with a cloud of low hit point airborne units such as Gargoyles or Flying Machines due to Bat Riders, but have less tools to deal with the likes of Frost Wyrms, Destroyers and Gryphon Riders.

How To Basic: Undead

Advantages

  • Excellent single target damage

    Undead is notorious for being great at bursting down targets one by one. The most obvious reason for this is the devastating combination of Death Coil and Frost Nova. It's, however, not the only reason, as Undead also have several other traits which assist bursting single targets.

  • Versatile scouting

    Rod of Necromancy offers a lot of versatility, allowing you to both creep efficiently, harass and scout. A must have in all matchups.

  • Strong base defense

    An Undead base with a good layout is nearly impenetrable for the enemy. Workers are hidden well in the back, the slow from your main structure and Nerubian towers makes both fighting and running away much more inconvenient for your opponent.

  • High skill ceiling

    Undead is not a race with a lot of flexibility for creative and innovative play styles, but they have a ton of room for refinement. If you micro well you can achieve an incredible amount and win fights that you, on paper, have no business of winning.

Disadvantages

  • Limited sustain

    Until you achieve Obsidian Statues you don't have much sustain. The Tomb of Relics contains no Clarity Potion for mana, nor items for healing like Scroll of Regeneration or Healing Salves.

  • Weak crowd control

    Undead rely heavily on Lich's Frost Nova for crowd control, which doesn't compare well to other races' options like Ensnare/Hex, Slow/Storm Bolt, and Entangle/Slow Poison.

  • Late dispel access

    Undead is the only race that doesn't have dispelling effects before tier 3, when they can get Destroyers. This can be a significant disadvantage against buff-heavy strategies.

How To Basic: Night Elves

Advantages

  • Efficient resource management

    Free healing and free mana regeneration makes Moon Wells arguably the best food-production building in the game. However, use the Moon Juice carefully as Night Elves lack alternative healing before Druid of the Claw's Rejuvenation.

  • Unit versatility

    Much like Human, Night Elf has a wide collection of viable units. This allows them to be very versatile and able to adapt to any given situation.

  • Early game advantage

    Creeping with the Ancient of War allows Night Elf to 'cheat-creep' strong creep camps very early on in the game, providing a nice edge in the early game.

  • Powerful items

    Both permanent items in the Night Elf shop (Orb of Venom and Staff of Preservation) are great, especially when paired with Moon Wells for healing staffed units.

Disadvantages

  • Limited support heroes

    While Night Elves have good starter heroes, they lack strong supporting heroes compared to other races. This often leads to them rarely getting a third hero, despite frequently reaching tier 3.

  • Weak late game

    Night Elves' late game is considered the weakest among all races. Their units are not ideal for big late game fights, and they usually only have 2 heroes, making them vulnerable to the tri-hero combinations of other races.
